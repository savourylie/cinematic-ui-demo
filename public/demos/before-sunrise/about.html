I believe the best design disappears into the experience.

Designer, storyteller, quiet observer of the in-between moments.

It started the way most things worth doing start — without a plan. I picked up a sketchbook in a city I was passing through, sat in a cafe that smelled like roasted chestnuts, and drew the view from the window. Not because I was a designer. Because the light was doing something I wanted to remember.

That impulse — to hold onto a feeling, to translate something fleeting into something you can revisit — became the thread that led me here. I studied visual communication, fell in love with typography, learned that a well-set paragraph could feel like a conversation and a poorly-set one could feel like a lecture.

Over the years I have worked with studios, startups, and independent makers. Each project taught me something different: patience from a year-long rebrand, courage from a three-day sprint, humility from the projects that didn't work. I learned that the best work happens when you stop trying to impress people and start trying to connect with them.

Now I work independently, choosing projects where the conversation matters as much as the outcome. I believe design should feel like walking into a room that was arranged just for you — not because it shouts your name, but because the light, the space, and the details all quietly say: you belong here.

When I am not designing, I am probably walking somewhere without a destination, reading something I will want to talk about later, or sitting in a cafe watching the afternoon light move across the table. Some habits never change.

2018

Started exploring design as a way to tell stories. Filled notebooks with sketches of storefronts and type specimens found on long walks through unfamiliar neighborhoods.

2019

First freelance project — a cafe website that felt like walking into the space. Learned that restraint is harder than decoration, and more rewarding.

2021

Joined a studio as a designer, learning to balance vision with craft. Discovered that collaboration sharpens ideas the way conversation sharpens thinking.

2023

Went independent, focusing on projects that matter. Chose fewer clients, longer conversations, and work that could breathe.

2025

Still here, still curious, still walking. Every project is a new city to explore, a new story to tell well.

"You know what's the wonderful thing about firsts? They're always memorable."

How I Work

Every project begins with listening. Before sketches, before wireframes, before any pixel is placed, I want to understand what the work needs to feel like. Not what it should look like — what it should feel like to the person experiencing it for the first time.

I work slowly and deliberately. Not because speed is impossible, but because rushing is the enemy of atmosphere. The details that make a design feel alive — the spacing between lines, the weight of a heading, the pause before a transition — those details only reveal themselves when you give them time.

I believe in collaboration as conversation. The best projects I have been part of were the ones where the client and I were genuinely thinking together, not performing roles. I will ask questions you might not expect. I will push back gently when something doesn't serve the work. And I will always explain my reasoning.

The goal is never to make something that looks impressive in a portfolio. The goal is to make something that someone out there will feel grateful exists — because it made their experience a little more human, a little more considered, a little more worth their time.

Thanks for staying this long. The conversation doesn't have to end here.
