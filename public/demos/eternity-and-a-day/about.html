We believe that the most important stories are the ones that take time — to write, to read, to understand. This journal exists for the patient reader: the one who knows that meaning accumulates slowly, like fog gathering over a winter harbor. We do not chase the news. We wait for the words to find their weight.

How We Began

It started with a notebook left on a bus seat in Thessaloniki. Inside, someone had written fragments — half-finished poems, observations about the light on the harbor, a list of words in a language we didn't recognize.

We never found the owner. But we understood the impulse: to save words from disappearing. To write them down before the fog takes them.

This journal began as an attempt to continue that notebook. To gather the writers, translators, and thinkers who believe that language is not a tool but a landscape — vast, foggy, and worth crossing slowly.

We publish essays, reflections, poems, and conversations. Our pace is quarterly — not because we lack urgency, but because we believe in the slow accumulation of meaning. Each issue is a single day. Each day contains an eternity.

Our writers come from the borderlands — geographically, linguistically, emotionally. They write about displacement and belonging, about the words that exist in one language but not another, about the landscapes that shape how we think.

Every boundary is also a meeting place.

We are not nostalgic. We are attentive. There is a difference. Nostalgia looks backward with longing. Attention looks at what is here — the fog, the harbor, the stranger on the bus — and finds it sufficient.
